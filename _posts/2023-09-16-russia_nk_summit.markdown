## What specific needs does each leader have that they hope to fulfill through this summit?
Through this summit, President Putin and Kim Jong-un hope to fulfill their specific needs. Putin, facing war against Ukraine, needs munitions to bolster his campaign. On the other hand, Kim Jong-un requires advanced military technology, as well as food, fuel, and cash to support his isolated nation. Both leaders see an opportunity in each other to fulfill these needs, and this summit serves as a platform to discuss and potentially address these concerns.

## What implications does this summit have for international relations, particularly in relation to the ongoing conflict in Ukraine?
This summit has significant implications for international relations, particularly in relation to the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. Putin's war against Ukraine has faced military failures and setbacks, and he needs munitions to bolster his campaign. Kim Jong-un's promise of support could provide Putin with the necessary resources for his war efforts. This potential cooperation between Russia and North Korea raises concerns for the international community, especially the United States, which has warned against potential arms deals between the two nations. The United States emphasizes the need for strong enforcement of sanctions against North Korea. This summit further complicates the already tense situation in Ukraine and could escalate the conflict even further.

## How does the summit signal a potential shift in the relationship between Russia and North Korea?
The summit between Putin and Kim Jong-un signals a potential shift in the relationship between Russia and North Korea. Despite their troubled history as partners, geopolitical tensions have brought them closer together. The leaders expressed their support for each other's fight against imperialism, signaling a unified front against the West. This unity and cooperation between Russia and North Korea could alter the dynamics of international relations, especially considering North Korea's nuclear ambitions and Russia's geopolitical interests. It is a signal that Russia and North Korea are willing to explore avenues for partnership, potentially changing the geopolitical landscape in the region.
